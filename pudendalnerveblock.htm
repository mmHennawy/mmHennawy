Pudendal Nerve Block

 

External Method

The forfinger is placed on the ischial spine (behind which runs the pudendal nerve) and a long needle is passed via the ishiorectal fossa. When needle point, spine and finger are in conjuction, 5 ml of anesthetic is injected. It is advisable to withdraw the plunger before injecting to make sure that the needle is not in a blood vessel.

Internal Method

The needle, preferably a guarded one can be passed per vaginam if the practitioner finds it easier
